City of Ottawa - Ville d'Ottawa

Canada's Capital - Architecture and Wildlife

Situated at the confluence of two powerful rivers and the historic Rideau Canal, Ottawa is home to over 1 million inhabitants and is the seat of Canada's federal government. A popular destination for tourists (estd. 22 million annually), there are a number of outdoor activities for visitors to partake in during every season. From Winterlude to Tulip Festival in the spring, Bluesfest and City Folk music festivals to the changing of the leaves in the fall. Ottawa also features a number of world-class galleries and museums to visit when the weather isn't co-operating!

Historically, Ottawa's skyline was stunted by bylaws requiring all buildings be built shorter than the 92 m Peace Tower of Parliament Hill. That law was repealed in 1965, however the city still adheres to a strict and complicated system of view planes that limit building heights. This has resulted in Ottawa having fewer tall buildings than other comparably sized Canadian cities.

Ottawa's Tallest Buildings Over 100 m

  1. Claridge Icon, Little Italy (143 m)
  2. Place de Ville - Tower C, Downtown (112 m)
  3. Minto Metropole, Westboro (108 m)
  4. The Dale - Tower 1, Mechanicsville (106 m)
  5. Le Parc, Manor Park (104 m)

Ottawa features a number of architecturally distinct buildings in various styles, mostly shaped by the presence of the federal government (for better or worse).

Notable Examples of Ottawa's Architecture

  • Parliament of Canada - Neo-Gothic/Victorian High Gothic
  • National Gallery of Canada, Canadian War Museum - Postmodern Architecture
  • Canadian Museum of Nature - Tudor-Gothic Revival
  • Lester B. Pearson Building - Brutalism
  • Delegation of the Ismaili Imamat - Islamic Modernism

Local Wildlife

Ottawa experiences a semi-continental climate, with warm humid summers, and very cold winters. Lovers of nature and animals may also be interested to know about some of the region's local wildlife that can be spotted in the various parks and hikes around the city.
