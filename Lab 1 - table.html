Wild Fauna of the National Capital Region
Animal Name Species Lives In Image
Northeastern Coyote Canis latrans Coyotes typically inhabit woodlands and grasslands, but with recent human developments encroaching on their natural habitat, they are increasingly found in suburban and urban environments.
Midland Painted Turtle Chrysemys picta marginata Painted turtles generally prefer calm, quiet fresh water. Coves, shores, and wetlands such as swamps and ponds are all places that these colourful shelled creatures can be found.
Rosy Maple Moth Dryocampa rubicunda Temperate deciduous forests, primarily maple trees (hence their name) but also oak trees are where these little moths call home. They can also be found in nearby suburban/urban landscapes.
Northern Leopard Frog Lithobates pipiens Leopard frogs are found in a wide range of habitats, including: ponds, swamps, marshes, streams, woodlands, and are found in suburban lawns and grassy areas in the summer months.
Northern Cardinal Cardinalis cardinalis A widespread and adaptable species, cardinals are found in woodlands, gardens, shrublands, and wetlands all across the Americas. Known for their bright red colouration, the males of the species are easily spotted when darting around local foliage.
